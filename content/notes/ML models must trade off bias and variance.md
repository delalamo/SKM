#### Summary
**ML models trained on property prediction (such as [[Fitness prediction|fitness prediction]]) must trade off bias and variance** (Fannjiang and [@fannjiang2023]). Bias here refers to consistent inaccuracy while variance refers to uncertainty. Datasets that are small but contextually relevant tend to produce ML models with more of the latter, while those that are larger (e.g., sequences for [[Protein language models|PLMs]]) tend to produce ML models with more of the former.
