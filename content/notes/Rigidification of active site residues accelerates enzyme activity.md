#### Summary
**Rigidification of active site residues accelerates enzyme activity.** This was shown in a [[Kemp eliminase]] where residues in the first and second shell surrounding the active site were mutated [^broom2020][^zarifi2025], as well as synthetic *de novo* designed enzymes [^hou2025].

[^broom2020]: Broom et al. (2020) "Ensemble-based enzyme design can recapitulate the effects of laboratory directed evolution in silico." *Nature Communications*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-18619-x
[^zarifi2025]: Zarifi et al. (2025) "Distal mutations enhance catalysis in designed enzymes by facilitating substrate binding and product release." https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.02.21.639315
[^hou2025]: Hou et al. (2025) "De novo design of porphyrin-containing proteins as efficient and stereoselective catalysts." *Science*. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adt7268
