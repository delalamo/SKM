#### Summary
**[[Biparatopic]] [[Antibodies|antibodies]] can induce [[Antigen clustering|antigen clustering]] beyond what is observed in monoparatopic, monospecific antibodies** [^weisser2023]. In this case, HER2 clustering was examined using the biparatopic antibody Zanidatamab, which has one arm from [[Trastuzumab]] and one from Pertuzumab; dosing with either of the latter two monospecific antibodies did not yield the same antigen clustering.

[^weisser2023]: Weisser et al. (2023) "An anti-HER2 biparatopic antibody that induces unique HER2 clustering and complement-dependent cytotoxicity." *Nature Communications*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-37029-3
