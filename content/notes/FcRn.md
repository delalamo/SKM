The **neonatal Fc receptor** (abbreviated as **FcRN**) is a heterodimeric receptor that binds the [[Fc-regions]] of [[tags/antibodies|antibodies]] at low pH in [[Endocytosis|endosomes]] but not at neutral pH (except IgG3). This is critical for IgG recycling responsible for long half-life. Schoch et al. [@schoch2015] showed that the [[Variable-regions|Fv-region]] (more specifically the light chain) also binds FcRn, and that slow dissociation can shorten antibody half-life.

#### Structure

FcRn is a heterodimeric protein with an glycosylated class I MHC-like protein alpha subunit, and an immunoglobulin beta subunit.
