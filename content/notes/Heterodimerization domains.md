**Heterodimerization domains** can be used to noncovalently link two different proteins together.

#### Examples

* Barnase/barstar ([10.3390/molecules26226785](https://doi.org/10.3390%2Fmolecules26226785)), a high-affinity protein pair used for exactly these purposes. That review puts the affinity (Kd) at the femtomolar level. Someone suggested introducing a mutation in the active site of barnase, which is an active ribonuclease, this paper ([doi.org/10.1021/bi00435a033](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi00435a033)) suggests H102A and E73A, but I’m unclear on whether that also prevents barstar binding. More research is needed.
* Colicin E9DNAse/Im9 Cognate Immunity Protein, which is also femtomolar binding affinity ([doi.org/10.1021/bi00042a005](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi00042a005)). Again, Colicin is an active enzyme, so some search for mutations may be necessary.
* Trypsin/BPTI and anhydrotrypsin/BPTI, also femtomolar ([https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.90.1.337](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.90.1.337)).
* Choice mutations in OMTKY3/SGBP allow the binding affinity to allegedly reach subfemtomolar affinity ([https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2013.07.029](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.febslet.2013.07.029))
* SpyCatcher/SpyTag ([doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49233-7](http://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49233-7))
* FKBP (12 kDa FK506 binding protein) and FRB, which is rapamycin-dependent ([[_Articles that need citations|citation needed]])
