**Heterodimerization domains** can be used to noncovalently link two different proteins together.

#### Examples

* Barnase/barstar [@shilova2021] is a high-affinity protein pair used for exactly these purposes. That review puts the affinity (Kd) at the femtomolar level. Someone suggested introducing a mutation in the active site of barnase, which is an active ribonuclease; Mossakowska et al. [@mossakowska1989] suggest H102A and E73A, but I’m unclear on whether that also prevents barstar binding. More research is needed.
* Colicin E9DNAse/Im9 Cognate Immunity Protein, which is also femtomolar in binding affinity [@wallis1995]. Again, Colicin is an active enzyme, so some search for mutations may be necessary.
* Trypsin/BPTI and anhydrotrypsin/BPTI, also femtomolar [@borjigin1993].
* Choice mutations in OMTKY3/SGBP allow the binding affinity to allegedly reach subfemtomolar affinity [@qasim2013].
* SpyCatcher/SpyTag [@fierle2019].
* FKBP (12 kDa FK506 binding protein) and FRB, which is rapamycin-dependent ([[_Articles that need citations|citation needed]])
