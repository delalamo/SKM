#### Summary
DNA substitutions during [[Somatic hypermutation|somatic hypermutation]] are spatially clustered. In other words, codons are more likely to get multiple nucleotide substitutions in the same codon than equivalent stretches of DNA in other parts of the genome. [^matsen2026] found that a "multihit correction" during parametrization of their [[Antibody language models|antibody language model]] could account for this phenomenon. This was achieved by training on out-of-frame immunoglobulin sequence stretches.
