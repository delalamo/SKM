**Nanobodies** are single-chain [[tags/antibodies|antibody]]-like molecules (albeit 12-15kDa, or 1/10th the size). They are found in camelids such as alpacas, as well as sharks. Nanobodies from the former are abbreviated as $V_{H}H$ and consist of a homodimeric heavy-only chain with two constant regions each, while those from the latter are abbreviated as $V_{NAR}$ and consist of five constant regions each. $V_{H}H$ structures are heavy chain-like and consist of three [[Complementarity-determining regions|CDR]] loops and an Ig domain. Camelid nanobodies account for most of the length diversity in [[Complementarity-determining regions#CDRH1|CDRH1]], which in humans and mice are almost all length 7 [@nowak2016]. Shark nanobodies lack [[Complementarity-determining regions#CDRH2|CDR2]] and two strands from the [[Framework region|framework]], and thus don't resemble either heavy or light chain in humans; they are "[[Antibody humanization|humanized]]" into single-domain light chains.

#### General properties

* **[[Nanobodies]] are more soluble than [[tags/antibodies|antibodies]]** [@gordon2023].
