**Programmed death-1** (abbreviated as **PD-1** and sometimes called **CD279**) is a receptor found on [[T cells]] and [[tags/b-cells|B cells]] that regulates immune responses. As an "immune checkpoint" it prevents autoimmune responses when it binds to [[PD-L1]]. This pathway is used by cancer cells, but can be blocked by immunotherapeutic [[tags/antibodies|antibodies]].
