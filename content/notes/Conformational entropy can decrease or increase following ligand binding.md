#### Summary
**Conformational entropy can increase or decrease following ligand binding** [^wankowicz2024][^corbella2023]. Variation in conformational entropy changes are due to whether the enthalpy effect of small molecule binding is enough to counteract the loss of entropy.

#### Details
The classical view of small molecule binding is that it sharpens the energy minima of enzymes. [^corbella2023] provide several examples of this, including triosephosphate isomerase, orotidine 5-monophosphate decarboxylase, glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase, and beta-phosphoglucomutase. However, there are examples of the opposite happening. In (cAMP-dependent protein kinase A), greater dynamics are observed with (NMR) following binding of ATP [^wang2019].

[^wankowicz2024]: Wankowicz & Fraser (2025) "Advances in uncovering the mechanisms of macromolecular conformational entropy." *Nature Chemical Biology*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41589-025-01879-3
[^corbella2023]: Corbella et al. (2023) "Loop dynamics and the evolution of enzyme activity." *Nature Reviews Chemistry*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41570-023-00495-w
[^wang2019]: Wang et al. (2019) "Globally correlated conformational entropy underlies positive and negative cooperativity in a kinase’s enzymatic cycle." *Nature Communications*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-019-08655-7
