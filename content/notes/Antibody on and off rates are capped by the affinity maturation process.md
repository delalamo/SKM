#### Summary
**[[Antibodies|Antibody]] on and off rates are capped by the [[Affinity maturation|affinity maturation]] process** [^foote1995]. Authors propose caps of $10^{5}$ to $10^{6}\ M^{-1}s^{-1}$ for $k_{on}$ and $10^{-3}$ to $10^{-4}\ s^{-1}$ for $k_{off}$, leading to a cap of $10^{-10}\ M$ for $k_{d}$, beyond which [[B cells]] would have no selective advantage. The on-rate cap is due to diffusion limits, while the off-rate is governed by antibody reabsorption and [[Endocytosis|endocytosis]].

[^foote1995]: Foote & Eisen (1995) "Kinetic and affinity limits on antibodies produced during immune responses." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.92.5.1254
