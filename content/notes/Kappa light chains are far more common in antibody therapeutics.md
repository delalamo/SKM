#### Summary
**[[tags/light-chains|Kappa light chains]] are more common in [[tags/antibodies|antibody]] therapeutics.** Only ten percent of antibody therapeutics have the lambda subtype light chains, compared to 35–40% of natural antibodies; [@raybould2023] determine that this is due to poor [[tags/antibody-developability|developability]], including large hydrophobic patches (see [[Lambda light chains are more hydrophobic]]) and longer [[Complementarity-determining regions|CDRH3]] loops. This has also been attributed to the larger proportion of kappa light chains in mice (95% of all light chains).
