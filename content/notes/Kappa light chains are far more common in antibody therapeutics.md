#### Summary
**[[Light chains|Kappa light chains]] are more common in [[Antibodies|antibody]] therapeutics.** Only ten percent of antibody therapeutics have the lambda subtype light chains, compared to 35–40% of natural antibodies; [^raybould2023] determine that this is due to poor [[Developability|developability]], including large hydrophobic patches (see [[Lambda light chains are more hydrophobic]]) and longer [[Complementarity-determining regions|CDRH3]] loops. This has also been attributed to the larger proportion of kappa light chains in mice (95% of all light chains).
