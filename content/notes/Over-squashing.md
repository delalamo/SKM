#### Summary
Over-squashing is a phenomenon in [[Graph neural networks]] where individual nodes are not sensitive to distant information in ways that cannot be ameliorated by increasing network depth (Di [^di2023]). The term was coined by [^alon2021].

[^di2023]: Di Giovanni et al. (2023) "On Over-Squashing in Message Passing Neural Networks: The Impact of Width, Depth, and Topology." https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2302.02941
[^alon2021]: Alon & Yahav (2020) "On the Bottleneck of Graph Neural Networks and its Practical Implications." https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2006.05205
