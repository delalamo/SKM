#### Summary
 **Functional proteins can emerge from large libraries of random sequences** [@keefe2001]. From a library of $6*10^{12}$ 80-residue sequences, four bind ATP. One binding at 100 nM to 10 µM affinity using a zinc ion coordinated by a CXXC motif. The exact experiment is described in [@keefe2001] and allows these random sequences to evolve.

#### Details
The authors estimate that 1 out of $10^{11}$ random sequences bind ATP, and that this ratio is similar to recovery of ATP-binding RNAs (find citation in paper).
