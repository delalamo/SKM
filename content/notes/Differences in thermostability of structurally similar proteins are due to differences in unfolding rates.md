#### Summary
**Differences in [[tags/thermostability|thermostability]] of proteins with the similar structures are due to differences in rates of unfolding, rather than folding** [@tzul2017; @cea2024]. [@tzul2017] observed this using fifteen thioredoxin proteins, 7 of which were generated by [[tags/ancestral-sequence-reconstruction|ancestral sequence reconstruction]], whi [@cea2024] saw this in HMPPK enzymes involved in synthesizing vitamin B12. In both cases, proteins folded at similar rates, but unfolded at rates that varied up to three orders of magnitude. The authors offer this as evidence supporting the principle of minimal frustration (see [[tags/evolution-and-natural-selection|Evolution and natural selection]]).
