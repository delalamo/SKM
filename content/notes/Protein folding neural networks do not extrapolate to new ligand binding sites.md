## Summary

**Protein folding neural networks do not extrapolate to new ligand binding sites** ([d'Oelsnitz et al 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-46356-y), [Dürr & Rothlisberger 2024](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.28.596236)). [[AlphaFold|AlphaFold2]] could not predict a new calcium-binding site in an engineered protein, and instead introduced a fictitious salt bridge ([d'Oelsnitz et al 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-46356-y)). Separately, neither [[AlphaFold|AlphaFold3]] nor [[RosettaFold|RosettaFold-AA]] could predict that a binding site prefers cobalt over copper ([Dürr & Rothlisberger 2024](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.05.28.596236)).
