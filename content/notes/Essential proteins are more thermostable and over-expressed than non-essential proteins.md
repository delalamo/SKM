#### Summary
**Essential proteins are more likely to be thermostable** (Leuenberger et al 2017[^leuenberger2017]) **and over-expressed** (Choi et al 2026[^choi2026]) **than non-essential proteins.** These results were obtained in *E. coli* and *Acinetobacter baylyi*, respectively. This points to varying degrees of [[Evolution and natural selection|evolutionary pressure]] being placed on proteins, depending on their impact on cellular fitness, and directly contradicts the principle of marginal thermostability.
