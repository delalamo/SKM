[[Antibodies]] are naturally glycosylated but as therapeutics are sensitive to the addition of more sugar groups.

#### Notes

* **Potential sites for N-linked glycosylation include NG and N-!P-S/T-N where !P is not Pro** ([[10.1038__s41551-023-01079-1|Tennenhouse et al 2023]]).
* **Potential sites for O-linked glycosylation include T-V-X-P and S/T-P-X-P where X is anything** ([[10.1016__s0006-3495(01)76074-2|Christlet & Veluraja 2001]]).
* **The glycosylation of [[Antibodies]] can either attenuate or worsen the immune response.** This depends on the person, their age, gender, pregnancy status, immune history. The amount ant type of glycans also has a huge impact ([[_Articles that need citations|citation needed]]).
* **Removal of glycans from IgG-antibodies [[Antibodies|antibodies]] increases flexibility of the molecule via the CH2 domain.** This causes tighter crystal packing while increasing radius of gyration. It also decreases [[Stability and thermostability|stability]]. Has no impact on [[FcRn]] binding, but does shorten half-life via increased Lectin-mediated clearance ([[_Articles that need citations|citation needed]]).
* **[[Fc-gamma-receptors]] cannot bind [[Antibodies]] without glycosylation unless they have specific mutations** ([[_Articles that need citations|citation needed]]).
