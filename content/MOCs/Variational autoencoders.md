**Variational autoencoders** (VAEs) are neural networks that learn a [[Dimensionality reduction|reduced representation]] of a set of inputs by compressing an input signal and trying to recover it in the presence of noise. **Vector-quantized variational autoencoders** (VQ-VAEs) discretize the inputs into one-hot representations, essentially creating an alphabet (or codebook) for possible input values. These can, however, be prone to "codebook collapse" ([[10.1101__2024.08.06.606920|Lu et al 2024b]]).
