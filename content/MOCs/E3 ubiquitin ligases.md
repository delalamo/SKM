**E3 ubiquitin ligases** are proteins that transfer [[Ubiquitin]] from an [[E2 ubiquitin-conjugating enzymes|E2 ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme]] to a target destined for degradation. They are one of the binding partners of [[PROTACs]] and [[Molecular glues]].

#### Details

Per [[10.1038__s41573-021-00371-6|Békés et al 2022]], there are many subtypes of E3 ubiquitin ligases (three of which are listed below):
1. RING: largest class, simultaneously binds E2-Ub and target
2. HCET: Transfer Ub to itself and then moves it to target
3. RBR: Hybrid strategy
