**Protein dynamics** is a catch-all term referring to the conformational heterogeneity of folded protein structures. Weber described them as "screaming and kicking". As is noted by [[10.1038__nature06522|Henzler-Wildman & Kern 2007]], dynamics are described by 1) timescale, 2) amplitude, and 3) directionality, and they distinguish between fast and slow dynamics based on whether the energy barrier between the two sampled states exceed 1 kT.

#### Notes

* **The variation among protein structures determined by [[X-ray-crystallography]] broadly reproduce the dynamics measured by [[NMR]]** ([[10.1073__pnas.0511156103|Best et al 2006]]). They also fit NMR data better than ensembles folded using those NMR data.
* ***De novo* designed proteins can also have conformational equilibria that are modified by point mutations** ([[10.1126__science.adr7094|Guo et al 2025]]).
* The free energy landscape model of protein dynamics was first proposed by [[10.1021__bi00695a021|Austin et al 1975]].
