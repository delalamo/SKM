**Somatic hypermutation** is the process by which the 40 [[Germline#V-genes|V-genes]], five [[Germline#J-genes|J-genes]], and 23 [[Germline#D-genes|D-genes]] are combined and evolved to better bind specific targets. The mechanism targets residues unevenly, favoring hotspots (including [[Complementarity-determining regions|CDRs]]), and also includes **V(D)J recombination**. [[10.1080__19420862.2016.1158370|Nowak et al 2016]] calculate that human organism can produce $10^{12}$ distinct [[Antibodies|antibodies]]. This happens after [[Class switching|class switching]] from IgM to IgG.
